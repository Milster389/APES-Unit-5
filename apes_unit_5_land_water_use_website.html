Section 1

Agriculture & Land Use

Agriculture occupies about 50% of Earth's habitable land and uses 70% of freshwater withdrawals globally. The way we farm profoundly shapes soil health, water quality, climate, and biodiversity.

🌱 Sustainable Farming Methods

  • Crop rotation — prevents soil nutrient depletion
  • Contour plowing — reduces erosion on slopes
  • Terracing — creates level planting areas on hillsides
  • No-till farming — preserves soil structure & carbon
  • Cover crops — protects soil between growing seasons
  • Integrated Pest Management (IPM) — minimizes pesticide use
  • Polyculture — growing multiple crops together

⚠️ Unsustainable Practices & Impacts

  • Monoculture — increases pest/disease vulnerability
  • Overuse of fertilizers → eutrophication
  • Pesticide runoff → bioaccumulation in food chains
  • Overgrazing → desertification and erosion
  • Slash and burn agriculture
  • Salinization from over-irrigation
  • Soil compaction from heavy machinery

🔑 The Green Revolution

The mid-20th century Green Revolution dramatically increased crop yields through high-yield variety seeds, synthetic fertilizers, pesticides, and mechanized farming. It reduced famine but also accelerated soil degradation, water use, and chemical pollution. Today's challenge is a doubly green revolution: high yields with lower environmental impact.

🌾 Land Degradation

🏜️

Desertification

Conversion of arid or semi-arid land to desert conditions due to overgrazing, deforestation, and poor land management.

💨

Soil Erosion

Removal of topsoil by wind or water. Accelerated by deforestation, plowing, and overgrazing.

🧂

Salinization

Buildup of salts in soil from excessive irrigation. Reduces crop yield and can eventually make soil infertile.

Section 2

Water Use & Management

Only 3% of Earth's water is fresh, and most of that is frozen in glaciers or ice caps. Humans rely on a small fraction for drinking, agriculture, and industry.

💧 Irrigation Methods Compared

  • Flood irrigation — cheap but inefficient; can cause salinization
  • Furrow irrigation — better than flood but still loses water
  • Sprinkler irrigation — more efficient, but evaporation still occurs
  • Drip/micro irrigation — most efficient; delivers water directly to roots

🏭 Water Use by Sector

  • Agriculture — largest global freshwater user
  • Industry — cooling, manufacturing, and energy
  • Domestic — drinking, sanitation, and household use
  • In the U.S., thermoelectric power is a major water withdrawer

⚠️ The Ogallala Aquifer

The Ogallala Aquifer underlies 8 Great Plains states and supplies a huge share of U.S. irrigation water. It is being pumped far faster than it naturally recharges, making it a key example of groundwater overdraft.

Section 3

Mining & Forestry

⛏️ Mining Methods

Surface Mining

Removes overlying rock or soil to reach mineral deposits near the surface.

  • Open-pit mining — large excavated pits
  • Strip mining — removes strips of land for coal
  • Mountaintop removal — blasts off summits for coal seams
  • Placer mining — uses water to separate minerals from sediment

Subsurface Mining

Accesses deep deposits through shafts and tunnels. Less land disturbance, but higher worker risk and possible subsidence.

  • Shaft mining — vertical tunnels to ore deposits
  • Slope mining — angled tunnels following an ore seam
  • Drift mining — horizontal tunnels in hillsides

🌲 Forestry Methods

🌲

Clear-Cutting

All trees in an area are removed at once. It is cheap and efficient, but causes habitat loss, erosion, and watershed damage.

🌳

Selective Cutting

Only specific trees are harvested. It maintains more forest structure and biodiversity but is more expensive.

🔥

Prescribed Burns

Controlled fires reduce fuel load, recycle nutrients, and restore fire-adapted ecosystems.

⚠️ Acid Mine Drainage

When mining exposes sulfide minerals to air and water, sulfuric acid forms and leaches heavy metals into nearby waterways. It can kill aquatic life and persist for decades after a mine closes.

Legislation

Key Laws & Policies

Surface Mining Control and Reclamation Act

USA · 1977

Regulates surface coal mining and requires companies to restore mined land after mining.

Clean Water Act

USA · 1972

Regulates pollutant discharges into U.S. waters and protects water quality.

Federal Land Policy and Management Act

USA · 1976

Guides how the Bureau of Land Management manages public lands using multiple-use principles.

Safe Drinking Water Act

USA · 1974

Protects public drinking water supplies and sets contaminant standards.

National Forest Management Act

USA · 1976

Requires management plans for national forests and promotes sustainable timber harvesting.

Soil and Water Conservation Act

USA · 1977

Directs national assessment and monitoring of soil, water, and related resources.

1. Which irrigation method is the MOST water-efficient and delivers water directly to plant roots?

✅ B) The Ogallala is being pumped faster than it naturally recharges.

5. The Surface Mining Control and Reclamation Act primarily requires mining companies to:

✅ B) SMCRA requires land reclamation after surface coal mining.
